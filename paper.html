What insights can be gained into the formation and growth of supermassive black holes by comparing and contrasting heavy seed and light seed models?

Abstract:

This paper explores the formation and growth of supermassive black holes (SMBHs) in the early universe. We investigate different scenarios for the initial seeds of SMBHs, including heavy and light seeds. These scenarios involve processes like merging smaller black holes, Lyman-Werner background fluctuations, and conditions in high-redshift quasar host progenitors. By comparing heavy seed and light seed models, we assess their advantages and disadvantages, and how they influence SMBH assembly. We also touch on the potential detection of gravitational waves from SMBH mergers and the future prospects for studying SMBH formation and growth. This research offers valuable insights into SMBHs' nature in the early universe.

What environment is favourable for heavy seed and light seed growth?

Submitted for publication review at Columbia Junior Science Journal

Abstract:

In this study, we investigate black holes and their interaction with matter, particularly using the Eddington accretion model. This model, named after Sir Arthur Eddington, helps us understand how black holes grow. We explore different scenarios for the origins of black holes, from massive star remnants to primordial entities. Our goal is to uncover the complex processes that govern black hole growth. We use a statistical tool called the root mean square approach to predict crucial parameters and gain insights into how black hole accretion works. Ultimately, our study aims to provide informed conclusions about black hole behavior within the Eddington model and contribute to our understanding of these cosmic enigmas.
